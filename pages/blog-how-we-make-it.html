From Tây Ninh to Your Door: How We Make Every Bag

We source every ingredient the same way the street carts do — no shortcuts, no substitutes. Here's what goes into every single bag of Ăn Là Nhớ.

People sometimes ask us what makes Ăn Là Nhớ taste different from the bánh tráng trộn they've tried before. The honest answer is: the ingredients. Not a secret recipe, not a proprietary process — just the right materials, sourced from the right place, treated with the same care a good street vendor gives every order.

It starts with the rice paper

Most bánh tráng sold outside of Tây Ninh is machine-pressed and thin — fine for spring rolls, but it turns soggy fast when mixed with wet ingredients. We use bánh tráng made the traditional way: thicker, with a slight smokiness from being dried over low heat. It tears cleanly into strips that stay chewy and hold their texture even after mixing.

Sourcing this takes work. We deal directly with small producers in Tây Ninh province — the same families that supply the local street markets — rather than buying from distributors who stock whatever is cheapest.

The shrimp salt is non-negotiable

Muối tôm is the ingredient that separates a Tây Ninh bánh tráng trộn from every imitation. It's made by fermenting tiny, fragrant shrimp with salt and chili, then sun-drying the mixture until it intensifies into a coarse, brick-red powder. The flavor is simultaneously briny, umami-forward, and subtly spicy.

Generic shrimp salt — the kind you find pre-packaged in Asian grocery stores across the US — doesn't come close. The terroir of the Vàm Cỏ shrimp, the specific fermentation method, the sun-drying time: all of it matters. We won't compromise on this ingredient.

Small batches, every time

Each bag is mixed by hand in small batches. This isn't nostalgia — it's quality control. When you mix too much at once, the ingredients distribute unevenly and the rice paper breaks down. Small batches mean every strip of rice paper gets properly coated, every piece of beef floss is evenly distributed, every bite tastes the same as the last.

The mixing itself takes time. You have to work fast enough that the rice paper doesn't absorb too much moisture, but slow enough to not break it. It's a skill that the street carts in Tây Ninh take years to develop. We take it seriously too.

"No shortcuts. No substitutes. The same ingredients the street carts use."

Sealed for freshness

Once mixed, each bag is sealed immediately to lock in the texture and aroma. We use food-safe packaging designed for snack products — airtight, with enough nitrogen flush to keep the rice paper from going stale during transit.

The goal is simple: when you open the bag, it should smell and taste like it was just mixed. Not like something that sat in a warehouse for three months.

Shipped across the US

We ship within 1–2 business days. Bánh tráng trộn is shelf-stable at room temperature — the dried ingredients are what make it possible to ship it at all without refrigeration. But freshness still matters: the sooner it arrives, the better it tastes. We're constantly optimizing our shipping routes to get orders out fast.

That's the whole process. No secret. Just good ingredients, handled well.
