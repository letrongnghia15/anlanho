The Product

What is bánh tráng trộn?

Bánh tráng trộn is a Vietnamese street snack made from dried rice paper torn into strips and hand-mixed with shrimp salt (muối tôm), beef floss (bò khô), shredded green mango, roasted peanuts, quail eggs, fresh herbs, chili, and lime. It originated in Tây Ninh province, where it's sold from street carts outside schools and markets every afternoon. Read the full explainer →

What does "chính gốc Tây Ninh" mean?

Chính gốc means original — authentic, from the source. Tây Ninh province is where bánh tráng trộn was born, and its shrimp salt and rice paper are unlike anything made elsewhere. When we say chính gốc, we mean our ingredients are sourced directly from Tây Ninh producers, not from generic suppliers. You can taste the difference.

How spicy is it?

It depends on the variant. Our Classic is medium heat — noticeable but not overwhelming. Extra Hot lives up to its name and is genuinely spicy. Veggie is mild. Each product page shows a spice meter so you can pick the right level. If you're new to the snack, start with Classic.

Is it ready to eat straight from the bag?

Yes — tear it open and eat. That's the whole point. For the full street-cart experience, you can add fresh lime juice, herbs (tía tô or rau răm), or soft-boiled quail eggs before eating. See 5 ways to level it up →

What are the full ingredients?

Each product has its own ingredient list — see the individual product pages in the shop for complete details. In general, all variants include: dried rice paper, shrimp salt, beef floss or veggie protein, roasted peanuts, green mango powder, chili, lime, and herbs. The Veggie variant uses no beef or seafood-derived ingredients.

Storage & Shelf Life

Does bánh tráng trộn need refrigeration?

No. The snack is fully shelf-stable at room temperature. Store it in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight or heat. Avoid humidity — moisture is the main threat to the rice paper texture.

What is the shelf life?

Unopened bags have a shelf life of 4–6 months from the production date. The best-by date is printed on each bag. Once opened, reseal tightly and consume within 2–3 days for the best texture — the rice paper will soften if exposed to air for too long.

Can I freeze it?

We don't recommend freezing. The texture of the rice paper changes significantly when frozen and thawed, and the mixed ingredients don't hold up well. Just store at room temperature in a sealed bag — it keeps well without refrigeration or freezing.

Allergens & Dietary

What allergens does it contain?

Our products contain one or more of the following allergens depending on the variant:

  • Shellfish (shrimp salt — present in all variants)
  • Peanuts (present in all variants)
  • Eggs (quail eggs — present in Classic, Extra Hot, Family Pack)
  • Soy (some variants)

Our facility handles all of these allergens. Individuals with severe allergies should check the full ingredient list on the product page before ordering.

Is it gluten-free?

Rice paper is naturally gluten-free. However, some seasoning components (beef floss, soy sauce) may contain wheat. We do not certify our products as gluten-free. If you have celiac disease or a severe gluten intolerance, please review the ingredient list on the product page carefully before purchasing.

Is it vegan or vegetarian?

Our Classic, Extra Hot, and Family Pack variants contain beef floss, shrimp salt, and quail eggs — they are not vegan or vegetarian. Our Veggie variant is made without beef or seafood-derived ingredients and is suitable for vegetarians. Check the product page for exact details.

Is it halal?

We do not currently hold halal certification. Some of our products contain pork-derived ingredients. Please check the individual product pages for full ingredient details, and contact us if you need additional documentation for dietary compliance.
